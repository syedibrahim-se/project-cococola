Our Story

Early Marketing and Expansion

Asa Candler, a businessman, bought the formula and brand rights in 1888 and founded The Coca-Cola Company. Under his leadership, Coca-Cola grew rapidly, and Candler's aggressive marketing tactics helped make Coca-Cola a household name.


The Birth of Coca-Cola

To avoid Atlanta's prohibition laws, the wine was replaced with sugar syrup, and on May 8, 1886, the drink was officially named "Coca-Cola." The term "Coca" came from the coca leaf (used to flavor the drink), and "Cola" from the kola nut (a source of caffeine).


Trademark and Bottling System

In 1892, Candler formed The Coca-Cola Company and obtained the exclusive rights to the Coca-Cola name and formula. He also began granting licenses to independent bottling companies, a strategy that helped Coca-Cola expand rapidly across the United States.


The Contour Bottle

In 1916, the iconic Coca-Cola bottle, designed by the Root Glass Company, was introduced. Its distinctive contour shape made it instantly recognizable and became a symbol of the brand.

Coca-Cola Goes Global

Coca-Cola began its international expansion in the early 20th century, and today it's available in nearly every country on Earth. It was a favorite among American soldiers during World War II, further spreading its global presence.


New Products

Over the years, Coca-Cola introduced various products and brand extensions, including Diet Coke, Sprite, Fanta, and Coca-Cola Zero Sugar, to cater to different consumer preferences.


Challenges and Innovations

The company has faced challenges and controversies over the years, such as the "New Coke" debacle in the 1980s. However, it has also continually adapted to changing consumer tastes and preferences through innovations and acquisitions, including buying brands like Honest Tea and smartwater.


Modern Era

Today, The Coca-Cola Company is a multinational corporation with a wide range of non-alcoholic beverage brands and products, including not only carbonated soft drinks but also water, juices, teas, coffees, and more.
